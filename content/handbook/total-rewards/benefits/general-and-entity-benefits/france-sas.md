## We've Got You Covered

**Clear benefits, real support**

At GitLab, we believe team member benefits should be simple to understand and there when you need them. From life's big moments to everyday questions, our comprehensive coverage gives you clarity and confidence so you can focus on your work and life.
In addition to the local benefits listed below, all France team members have access to the [company-wide benefits](/handbook/total-rewards/benefits/general-and-entity-benefits/) available to all team members globally.

## Health & Wellbeing

### Supplementary Medical Insurance

GitLab provides comprehensive supplementary medical insurance through AXA Health Insurance to enhance your healthcare coverage beyond the French social security system. This valuable coverage includes medical, vision, dental, and maternity benefits designed to support your health and wellness needs.

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Eligibility Requirements</summary>

- All GitLab France S.A.S. team members
- Coverage begins from your hire date
- Spouse and children eligible for dependent coverage

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Coverage & Key Features</summary>

- 100% employer-paid coverage for team members
- 66% employer contribution for dependent premiums
- Comprehensive medical, vision, and dental coverage
- Maternity and preventive care benefits
- [View full policy details](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1xfkzQXwiITrhJ0HJk9K-eTtSjLEONiNB/view?usp=sharing) and [additional coverage information](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1lKcSkHJKpOaJwVFUgCQDWgOHyFRkSib8/view?usp=sharing)

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>How to Enroll/Access</summary>

- Watch for enrollment email from no-reply@axa.fr within your first two weeks
- Follow the instructions in the email to complete enrollment
- Review the [enrollment process guide](https://drive.google.com/file/d/11JmUP7lVhzyqO0QJGfEr4YpYLH7vbMb6/view?usp=sharing) for step-by-step assistance
- Access the [Unilateral Employer Decision Document](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1qPpQqg9WZsDaF8lxhXIc4tPEt5GbJc8R/view?usp=sharing) for cost sharing details

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Important Dates & Details</summary>

- Enrollment window: First two weeks from hire date
- Coverage effective: Date of hire

</details>

## Financial Security

### Holiday Bonus

GitLab provides an annual Holiday Bonus to recognize your contributions and support your financial wellbeing. This valuable benefit adds meaningful value to your total compensation package.

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Eligibility Requirements</summary>

- All GitLab France S.A.S. team members
- Must be employed during June to receive bonus
- Based on prior 12 months of employment (June 1 - May 31)

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Key Features</summary>

- 1% of total gross compensation from previous 12 months
- Includes base salary, bonuses, commissions, and overtime
- Subject to social charges and income tax
- Paid automatically - no enrollment required

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Payment Details</summary>

- Payment date: June payroll each year
- Calculation period: June 1 through May 31

</details>

### Retirement Planning

While GitLab doesn't currently offer supplementary pension benefits, all team members are automatically enrolled in France's comprehensive Social Security Program, including the National Pension Scheme, providing retirement security aligned with French standards.

## Growing Family Leaves

GitLab France offers comprehensive parental leave benefits that meet or exceed statutory requirements. Whether you're welcoming your first child or growing your family, we provide the time and financial support you need during this important life transition.

### Maternity Leave

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Eligibility Requirements</summary>

- All pregnant team members
- Coverage begins from hire date
- Must submit leave request 30 days in advance

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Leave Entitlement</summary>

- First or second child: 16 weeks (6 weeks before birth, 10 weeks after)
- Third child: 26 weeks (8 weeks before birth, 18 weeks after)
- Twins: 34 weeks (12 weeks before birth, 22 weeks after)
- Triplets or more: 46 weeks (24 weeks before birth, 22 weeks after)
- GitLab supplements social security benefits to provide 100% salary for first 16 weeks
- [Review eligibility for social security benefits](https://www.ameli.fr/assure/remboursements/indemnites-journalieres/conge-maternite-salariee)

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>How to Apply</summary>

1. Submit "Parental Leave" request in Workday at least 30 days before leave start date
2. Review [GitLab's Parental Leave policy](/handbook/total-rewards/benefits/general-and-entity-benefits/#parental-leave)
3. Apply for social security benefits as applicable
4. Provide required documentation to leaves@gitlab.com

</details>

### Paternity Leave

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Eligibility Requirements</summary>

- All team members after birth of child
- Includes births to spouse, common-law partner, or PACS partner
- Must submit leave request 30 days in advance

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Leave Entilement</summary>

- Total leave: 28 days (35 days for multiple births)
- Structure: 3 days mandatory at birth + 4 days immediately following + 21 additional days
- 21-day period can be split into two segments (minimum 5 days each)
- GitLab pays 100% salary for first 3 days
- GitLab supplements social security benefits to provide 100% salary for first 16 weeks
- [Review eligibility for social security benefits](https://www.ameli.fr/assure/remboursements/indemnites-journalieres/conge-paternite-accueil-enfant)

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>How to Apply</summary>

1. Submit "Parental Leave" request in Workday at least 30 days before leave start date
2. Review [GitLab's Parental Leave policy](/handbook/total-rewards/benefits/general-and-entity-benefits/#parental-leave)
3. Apply for social security benefits as applicable
4. Provide required documentation to leaves@gitlab.com

</details>

## Time Off Policies

### Paid Time Off (Vacation)

GitLab France provides generous paid time off in accordance with French statutory requirements, plus additional RTT (Reduction of Working Time) days. This comprehensive approach ensures you have time to rest, recharge, and maintain work-life balance.

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Eligibility Requirements</summary>

All GitLab France S.A.S. team members
Entitlement based on French labor law
Must track time in both Workday and Mihi

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Key Features</summary>

- Statutory vacation days per French requirements
- Additional RTT days for work-life balance. Team members are responsible for ensuring that they are tracking their time accurately, both in Mihi and in Workday.
- Flexible time off through GitLab's PTO policy
- Team members can review [statutory requirements](/handbook/people-group/time-off-and-absence/time-off-types/) in relation to vacation
- Team members should be aware of their applicable leave categories and can [review logging requirements](https://internal.gitlab.com/handbook/finance/payroll/timekeeping/france/#logging-time-away) for logging time away from work and the suggested order for using leave balances (internal access only)

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>How to Request Time Off</summary>

- Submit request in Workday via Slack
- Log time away in [Mihi Platform](https://2.mihi.info/Account/Login?ReturnUrl=%2F)
- For RTT: Select "RTT" in Mihi and "PTO" in Workday with comment noting "RTT"
- Ensure accurate tracking in both systems

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Important Details</summary>

- Both Mihi and Workday entries required
- RTT tracked separately from standard PTO

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Contact Information</summary>

- Technical support (Mihi): support@mihi.info
- Policy questions: leaves@gitlab.com

</details>

### Sick Leave Policy

When illness strikes, GitLab provides comprehensive sick leave support through a combination of French social security benefits and company salary supplementation, ensuring financial stability during health challenges.

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Eligibility Requirements</summary>

First 6 months of sick leave:

- 150 hours worked in prior 3 months, OR
- Salary of 1,015 times hourly SMIC in prior 6 months

Beyond 6 months:

- 12 months of social security contributions
- 600 hours worked in prior 12 months, OR
- Income of 2,030 times hourly SMIC in prior 12 months

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Coverage & Key Features</summary>

- Up to 6 months sick leave (extendable based on eligibility)
- GitLab supplements social security to provide 100% salary for first 25 working days
- After 25 days: Social security benefit of 50% daily wage (max â‚¬50.58/day)
- [Review current sick time policies](https://employment-social-affairs.ec.europa.eu/policies-and-activities/moving-working-europe/eu-social-security-coordination/your-rights-country-country/france_en)

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>How to Report Sick Leave</summary>

1. Notify GitLab within 48 hours via "Out Sick" in Workday
2. Enter sick time in Mihi platform
3. Send sheet no. 3 from physician to leaves@gitlab.com
4. Submit sheets no. 1 & 2 to social security organization
5. For extensions: Notify within 48 hours with updated sheet no. 3

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Important Details</summary>

- Notification deadline: 48 hours
- Medical documentation required
- GitLab salary supplement: First 25 working days

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Contact Information</summary>

Sick leave reporting and policy questions: leaves@gitlab.com

</details>
