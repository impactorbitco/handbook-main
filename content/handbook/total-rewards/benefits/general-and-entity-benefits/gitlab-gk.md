**Clear benefits, real support**

At GitLab, we believe team member benefits should be simple to understand and there when you need them. Our Japan benefits work with the country's statutory system to support you through life's biggest moments and everyday needs.

## Health & Wellbeing Benefits

<details>
<summary><strong>Medical Coverage</strong></summary>

**Overview**

GitLab team members are covered through Japan's Social Insurance system, managed by the National Health Insurance Association (Kyokai Kenpo). This provides comprehensive medical coverage from day one.

**Eligibility**

- All GitLab GK (Japan) team members
- Coverage effective from hire date

**Key Features**

Coverage includes hospital care, outpatient services, prescription medications, and specialist treatments with standardized 30% co-payments.

**How to Access Your Benefits**

1. **Receive Your Insurance Card**: Provided during onboarding
2. **Visit Healthcare Providers**: Present your card at participating facilities
3. **Pay Co-payments**: 30% at time of service

**Important Information**

- Managed by Kyokai Kenpo
- For details, see the [Leaflet section of the Kyokai Kenpo website](https://www.kyoukaikenpo.or.jp/event/kohoshizai/20220411/)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Annual Health Check-Up</strong></summary>

**Overview**

You're entitled to a comprehensive annual health check-up through your Social Insurance benefits.

**Eligibility**

- All team members covered by Kyokai Kenpo
- Available annually

**Key Features**

## Financial Security Benefits

<details>
<summary><strong>Employee Pension System</strong></summary>

**Overview**

Automatic enrollment in Japan's Employee Pension system provides retirement income from age 60.

**Eligibility**

- All team members registered for Social Insurance
- Requires 25+ years of contributions for full benefits

**Key Features**

Benefits calculated using salary history and contribution period. Contributions automatically deducted from salary.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Unemployment Insurance (Koyou Hoken)</strong></summary>

**Overview**

Government-managed unemployment insurance supports all team members with comprehensive benefits during job transitions.

**Eligibility**

- Working at least 20 hours per week
- Expected employment of 31+ days
- Includes international team members

**Key Features**

Covers unemployment benefits, childcare leave allowance, nursing care leave allowance, and education/training support.

</details>

## Family & Life Transition Support

<details>
<summary><strong>Maternity Leave & Benefits</strong></summary>

**Overview**

Take 14 weeks of protected maternity leave with two-thirds of your base salary through government allowance.

**Eligibility**

- All pregnant GitLab GK team members
- Available regardless of employment length

**Key Features**

Leave starts 6 weeks before due date. Government provides 2/3 salary during entire leave period.

**How to Access**

1. **Plan**: Leave begins 6 weeks before delivery
2. **Apply**: Submit government allowance application
3. **Coordinate**: Work with manager and People Operations

**Important Information**

- May coordinate with [GitLab Parental Leave](/handbook/people-group/time-off-and-absence/) if eligible

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Parental Leave (Child Care Leave)</strong></summary>

**Overview**

Take up to one year of protected leave with substantial government financial support.

**Eligibility**

- Available until child's first birthday
- Fixed-term employees need one year employment
- Must commit to return to work

**Key Features**

Receive 67% salary (first 6 months), 50% thereafter. Both parents can take leave.

**How to Access**

1. **Plan**: Starts after maternity leave (mothers) or from birth (fathers)
2. **Apply**: Submit allowance application
3. **Coordinate**: Arrange role coverage

**Important Information**

- May coordinate with [GitLab Parental Leave](/handbook/people-group/time-off-and-absence/) if eligible

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Additional Family Support</strong></summary>

**Child Nursing Care Leave**: Up to 5 days (1 child) or 10 days (2+ children). Runs with GitLab PTO.

**Family Care Leave**: Up to 10 days annually for sick/injured family members. Runs with GitLab PTO.

**Nursing Care Leave**: Up to 93 days for family member nursing care, with potential government allowance.

</details>

## Time Off & Leave Policies

<details>
<summary><strong>Annual/Vacation Leave</strong></summary>

**Overview**

Guaranteed annual leave starting at 10 days after six months, increasing to maximum 20 days.

**Key Features**

Leave increases with tenure. Unused leave carries over up to two years.

**How to Access**

1. **Track**: Monitor through Workday
2. **Request**: Submit as "Paid Time Off" in Workday

**Important Information**

- No payment for unused leave upon termination

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Sick Leave</strong></summary>

**Overview**

Multiple support layers including job protection and income replacement through Japan's Health Insurance Scheme.

**Key Features**

- First 25 working days administered under GitLab's Sick Time policy
- Additional paid time can be administered under:
  - Accrued vacation (optional)
  - 60% salary from Japan's government Health Insurance Scheme
- Per GitLab's work rules, team members with less than 5 years of service are entitled to 3 months of job protected leave

**How to Access**

1. **Report**: Mark "Out Sick" in Workday
2. **Extended Leave**: For 5+ days, follow [Communicating Illness-Related Leave](/handbook/people-group/time-off-and-absence/time-off-types/#communicating-illness-related-leave) process
   - **Sick Leaves beyond 25 working days**: Team members to apply [here](https://www.ibmjapankenpo.jp/eng/member/application/rest_b.html)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Other Protected Leave</strong></summary>

**Menstrual Leave**: Unpaid leave when menstruation significantly impacts work ability.

**Civil Rights Leave**: Unpaid time for voting, jury service, and other civil duties.

</details>
