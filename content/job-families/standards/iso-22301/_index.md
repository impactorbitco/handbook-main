# **EVENT SUSTAINABILITY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (ESMS)**

Implemented November 2024  | Version 2.0

Comprehensive

(Aligned with BS ISO 20121:2012)

1. Purpose and Scope

The ESMS aims to provide a structured approach to managing the environmental, social, and economic impacts of events. It applies to all stages of the event lifecycle, including planning, implementation, and post-event activities.

- --

2. Principles

The system is underpinned by:

Inclusivity: Ensuring fair representation and engagement of all stakeholders.

Integrity: Maintaining ethical standards and accountability.

Stewardship: Minimising environmental impact and fostering long-term sustainability.

Transparency: Open communication about decisions, impacts, and performance.

- --

3. Leadership and Governance

Top Management Commitment: Leadership will ensure sustainability policies are integrated into event management processes, allocate resources, and lead by example.

Defined Roles and Responsibilities:

Event Sustainability Manager: Oversees implementation and compliance.

Stakeholder Liaison: Ensures continuous communication and feedback with stakeholders.

Operational Leads: Manage sustainability initiatives across functions.

- --

4. Policy

A documented Event Sustainability Policy is the foundation of the system, addressing:

Sustainable procurement and supply chain management.

Community engagement and legacy considerations.

Compliance with legal, ethical, and sustainability standards.

- --

5. Planning

5.1 Risk and Opportunity Assessment

Identify risks and opportunities related to environmental, social, and economic impacts.

Evaluate alternatives to minimise risks and maximise benefits.

5.2 Objectives and Targets

Set SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) sustainability objectives, e.g.:

Reduce waste by 50% through recycling and composting.

Achieve 75% attendee use of public transport or carpooling.

Procure 80% of materials locally or sustainably sourced.

5.3 Legal and Other Requirements

Maintain a register of applicable laws, regulations, and voluntary commitments.

- --

6. Operations

6.1 Supply Chain Management

Include sustainability criteria in supplier selection.

Collaborate with suppliers to meet sustainability targets.

Regularly audit suppliers’ practices.

6.2 Venue and Logistics

Select venues with sustainable certifications or practices (e.g., renewable energy, efficient waste management).

Use energy-efficient equipment and minimise transportation emissions.

6.3 Resource Management

Prioritise renewable and recyclable materials.

Reduce water and energy consumption through monitoring and optimisation.

6.4 Accessibility and Inclusivity

Ensure the event is accessible to all, including individuals with disabilities.

Promote diversity and inclusion in event planning and participation.

6.5 Emergency and Contingency Planning

Prepare for unforeseen circumstances, including environmental disasters or social disruptions.

- --

7. Stakeholder Engagement and Communication

7.1 Identification and Engagement

Identify key stakeholders: attendees, suppliers, local communities, government bodies, etc.

Develop a stakeholder engagement plan to gather input and share progress.

7.2 Communication Plan

Internal: Regular updates via newsletters, meetings, and intranet.

External: Reports, press releases, and social media updates.

- --

8. Monitoring and Evaluation

8.1 Performance Measurement

Develop Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), e.g., carbon footprint per attendee, waste diversion rates.

Use tools like life cycle assessment (LCA) and carbon calculators.

8.2 Audits and Reviews

Conduct internal and external audits to ensure compliance and identify improvements.

Perform post-event evaluations to capture lessons learned.

- --

9. Documentation and Records

Maintain detailed records for transparency and accountability, including:

Sustainability plans and policies.

Legal compliance registers.

Performance data and audit reports.

- --

10. Continuous Improvement

10.1 Feedback Loops

Gather feedback from stakeholders after every event.

Incorporate lessons learned into future planning.

10.2 Innovation and Best Practices

Research and adopt emerging technologies and practices.

Benchmark against industry leaders to stay competitive.

- --

11. Legacy Management

Evaluate long-term impacts and benefits, such as:

Positive economic contributions to local communities.

Enhanced environmental awareness among attendees.

Infrastructure or knowledge improvements for future events.

- --

Implementation Timeline and Milestones

1. Month 1-3: Develop policies and identify key stakeholders.

2. Month 4-6: Conduct baseline assessments and set

objectives.

3. Month 7-12: Implement actions, monitor progress, and audit systems.

# **1. Scope of the Quality Management System (QMS)**

- We consistently meet customer expectations for quality, innovation, and environmental sustainability.
- All applicable legal, statutory, and regulatory requirements are fulfilled.

Our QMS applies to all aspects of our operations, including client communication, service development, project delivery, stakeholder engagement, and performance evaluation. By addressing these areas, the QMS:

- Ensures that resources are used efficiently.

The QMS is applicable to all employees, external providers, and stakeholders involved in Green Orbit Digital’s services.
