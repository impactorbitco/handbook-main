# Event Logging and Review Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a framework for the logging, monitoring, and reviewing of events within the organization to ensure compliance with the ISO/IEC 27001: Information Security Management System standards. This policy aims to enhance organizational security, provide accountability, and support incident response activities.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties involved in the management, use, or access to the organization's information systems. It encompasses all event logging activities related to physical and digital assets as defined under the Annex A8 Control Group.

## Policy Statement

The organization commits to maintaining a robust event logging and review process to ensure compliance with international standards for information security. Through systematic monitoring and review, the organization will identify and address potential security threats, support forensic investigations, and ensure accountability for the use of organizational resources.

### Objectives

- **Ensure Event Logging**: All critical systems and physical environments will have logging mechanisms in place to capture relevant user activities and system events.
- **Timely Review**: Logs will be reviewed regularly to detect anomalies, security incidents, or breaches.
- **Access Controls**: Log data will be protected against unauthorized access, modification, or deletion.
- **Incident Response Support**: Logged events will provide detailed information to support the investigation of security incidents.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Owner**: Ryan Laird is responsible for overseeing the implementation and maintenance of this policy.
- **Employees**: Employees are required to comply with logging practices as outlined in this policy.
- **IT and Security Teams**: These teams are responsible for ensuring the technical implementation of logging systems and conducting regular reviews of event logs.

## Logging Requirements

1. **Minimum Information to Log**:
    - User actions (e.g., logins, logouts, and administrative activities)
    - System events (e.g., system errors, configuration changes)
    - Physical access logs for secure areas
2. **Retention Periods**:
    - Logs will be retained for a minimum of 12 months unless otherwise specified by legal or regulatory requirements.
3. **Log Protection**:
    - Logs will be stored in secured locations, with access restricted to authorized personnel only.

## Review and Reporting

- **Review Frequency**: Logs will be reviewed at least quarterly by the IT and Security Teams, or more frequently if required by the Priority Level or risk assessments.
- **Incident Escalation**: Any identified security breaches or anomalies will be escalated to management and investigated according to the organization’s incident response plan.
