## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a strategic direction for the secure transfer of information within the organization and with external parties. This policy aligns with the organization's commitment to information security and compliance with the ISO/IEC 27001: Information Security Management System.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties involved in the transfer of information, whether internal or external to the organization. It governs all types of information, including electronic, physical, and verbal communication.

## Policy Statement

The organization is committed to ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information during transfer processes. All information transfers, regardless of format, must follow established security protocols to mitigate risks and comply with applicable standards and regulations.

## Objectives

1. To protect sensitive information from unauthorized access, disclosure, or modification during transfer.
2. To ensure compliance with the ISO/IEC 27001 standard and other relevant regulatory requirements.
3. To establish clear roles and responsibilities for individuals involved in information transfer.
4. To ensure that all information transfer processes are documented, monitored, and reviewed for effectiveness.

## Responsibilities

- **Owner:** Ryan Laird is responsible for overseeing and maintaining this policy.
- **Employees and Contractors:** All personnel must adhere to the guidelines outlined in this policy when transferring information.
- **Information Security Team:** The team must verify that appropriate mechanisms are in place to secure information during transfer and ensure compliance with the policy.

## Guidelines for Information Transfer

1. **Internal Transfers:**
    - Use secure channels such as encrypted email or secure file transfer protocols (SFTP).
    - Verify the authenticity of recipients before sharing sensitive information.
    - Limit access to information on a need-to-know basis.
2. **External Transfers:**
    - Use approved tools and platforms for sharing information with external parties.
    - Establish non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) where applicable.
    - Conduct a risk assessment before engaging with third parties to evaluate potential risks related to information sharing.
3. **Physical Transfers:**
    - Store physical documents securely during transit (e.g., locked containers).
    - Use trusted couriers for delivering sensitive information.
    - Maintain a record of all physical transfers for auditing purposes.
4. **Verbal Communication:**
    - Avoid discussing sensitive information in public or unsecured areas.
    - Use secure communication tools for remote or virtual meetings.

## Compliance and Monitoring

- The organization will regularly review and update this policy to ensure it remains relevant and effective.
- Periodic audits will be conducted to assess compliance with the policy.
- Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract.
