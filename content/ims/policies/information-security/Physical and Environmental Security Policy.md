## Introduction

The Physical and Environmental Security Policy establishes the organization’s commitment to safeguarding physical and environmental aspects of its operations to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets. This policy aligns with the ISO/IEC 27001: Information Security Management System standards and is classified as a Level 1 – Strategic Direction document under the organization's hierarchy.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who have access to the organization's facilities, information systems, and physical assets. It also includes all locations where the organization operates or stores information assets.

## Objective

The primary objective of this policy is to protect the organization’s physical and environmental resources from unauthorized access, damage, or disruptions. This includes implementing measures to:

- Prevent unauthorized physical access to facilities and sensitive areas.
- Protect against environmental threats, such as fire, flooding, and other natural or man-made hazards.
- Ensure the resilience of critical operations and technology infrastructure.

## Responsibilities

### Policy Owner

Ryan Laird is the designated owner of this policy and is responsible for its implementation, review, and updates.

### All Employees

All employees are expected to comply with this policy and report any physical or environmental security concerns to their supervisor or the designated security team.

### Security Team

The security team is responsible for conducting regular risk assessments, audits, and monitoring to ensure compliance with this policy.

## Policy Details

### 1. Physical Access Controls

- Access to sensitive areas must be restricted to authorized personnel only.
- Security mechanisms such as keycards, biometric systems, or secure entry codes must be implemented and regularly updated.
- Visitor access must be logged, monitored, and supervised at all times.

### 2. Environmental Controls

- Adequate fire suppression systems, such as fire extinguishers and sprinkler systems, must be installed and maintained.
- Environmental monitoring systems must be deployed to detect and mitigate risks such as temperature fluctuations, flooding, and power outages.
- Backup power systems, including uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) and generators, must be available to ensure operational continuity.

### 3. Asset Protection

- All physical assets, including servers, networking equipment, and storage devices, must be securely housed in controlled environments.
- Equipment must be regularly inspected and maintained to prevent failures.
- Disposal of physical assets must be performed securely to prevent unauthorized access to sensitive information.

### 4. Incident Management

- Any physical or environmental incidents must be reported immediately and documented according to the organization's incident management procedures.
- Lessons learned from incidents will be used to update and improve this policy as needed.

## Compliance and Verification
