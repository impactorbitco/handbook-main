## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines and outline the acceptable use of company systems, ensuring the protection of information assets and compliance with applicable standards and regulations, including ISO/IEC 27001.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who have access to company systems and are responsible for adhering to the acceptable use standards outlined in this document.

## Policy Statement

The use of company systems must align with the organization's strategic direction and adhere to established information security policies and procedures. Any misuse of these systems may result in disciplinary action or termination of access.

## Acceptable Use Guidelines

1. **Authorized Access**: Users must only access systems, resources, or data for which they have been authorized. Sharing access credentials is strictly prohibited.
2. **Data Protection**: Users must adhere to data classification standards and ensure sensitive information is handled securely and in compliance with ISO/IEC 27001.
3. **Prohibited Activities**: Users may not engage in activities that:
    - Violate laws or regulations.
    - Compromise the security or integrity of company systems.
    - Include unauthorized downloading, installation, or use of software.
    - Transmit offensive, harassing, or discriminatory content.
4. **Use of Physical Assets**: Users must ensure that physical assets, such as hardware and removable media, are protected in line with Annex A8 Control Group standards.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Policy Owner**: Ryan Laird is responsible for maintaining and updating this policy (IMS ref.: ISMS/A7/POL/1892).
- **Employees and Users**: All users must comply with this policy and report any violations or security incidents.
- **Information Security Team**: Responsible for monitoring compliance and conducting periodic reviews of system usage.
