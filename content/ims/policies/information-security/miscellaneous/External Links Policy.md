## Overview

The External Links Policy outlines the organization's approach to managing the use of external links in its information systems, websites, and other communication channels. This policy is aligned with ISO/IEC 27001 standards to support the organization's commitment to information security and ensure compliance with international best practices.

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to regulate and manage the use of external links to ensure they do not compromise the integrity, security, or reputation of the organization. It is designed to mitigate risks associated with linking to external content, including potential exposure to malicious websites, misinformation, or unauthorized access.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, third-party vendors, and any individuals or entities who interact with the organization's systems and content. It covers all platforms where external links are used, including but not limited to websites, emails, documents, and intranet portals.

## Policy Statement

1. **Approval of External Links**:

    All external links must be reviewed and approved by the appropriate management function before being published. Links should align with the organization's strategic direction and information security policies.

2. **Verification of External Links**:

    All external links must be verified to ensure their legitimacy and security. Links that redirect to untrusted or suspicious domains are strictly prohibited.

3. **Regular Monitoring**:

    External links must be periodically reviewed to ensure they remain valid and relevant. Broken or outdated links should be removed promptly.

4. **Prohibited Links**:

    Links to websites containing malicious software, inappropriate content, or content that does not align with the organization's mission, vision, and values are prohibited.

5. **Employee Responsibility**:

    Employees are required to exercise caution when sharing or embedding external links. Any suspicious activity or security concerns related to external links should be reported to the security management function immediately.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Owner**: Ryan Laird is responsible for the overall management and oversight of this policy.
- **Management Functions**: The Security Management team (Mgt code: SEC) is tasked with implementing and enforcing this policy.
- **Review and Updates**: This policy will be reviewed regularly and updated as necessary to reflect changes in organizational needs or evolving security threats.

## Compliance and Enforcement

Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or termination of vendor contracts. The organization reserves the right to take legal action if necessary to protect its interests.
