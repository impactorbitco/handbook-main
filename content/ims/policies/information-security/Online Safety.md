# Online Safety Policy

## Introduction

This document outlines the Online Safety Policy (ID: 913) in alignment with the organization's commitment to ensuring information security and promoting good health and well-being as part of its strategic direction. It is classified under ISO/IEC 27001 standards, specifically addressing A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security. This policy is designed to guide the organization in maintaining a secure online environment while supporting Sustainable Development Goal 03 (Good Health and Well-being).

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a framework for protecting the organization's information assets against online threats. It aims to safeguard sensitive data, mitigate risks related to online interactions, and ensure compliance with international standards such as ISO/IEC 27001.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who interact with the organization's online systems. It covers all aspects of online safety, including but not limited to:

- Protection of sensitive information
- Safe use of online platforms and tools
- Identification and reporting of online threats
- Preventive measures against cyber risks

## Responsibilities

### Owner and Management

The policy owner, Ryan Laird, is responsible for overseeing the implementation and continuous improvement of this policy. The Security Management Function (code: SEC) will ensure that the policy is integrated into the organization's strategic direction and that all employees are aware of their responsibilities.

### Employees

All employees must adhere to the guidelines outlined in this policy, participate in training sessions, and report any online safety incidents to the appropriate authorities.

## Policy Guidelines

1. **Information Security**:
    - Adhere to the standards set forth in ISO/IEC 27001.
    - Implement strong access controls and encryption to protect sensitive data.
2. **Safe Online Practices**:
    - Use only approved online platforms and tools for organizational purposes.
    - Avoid sharing sensitive or confidential information over unsecured networks.
3. **Risk Management**:
    - Conduct regular risk assessments to identify potential online vulnerabilities.
    - Develop and maintain an incident response plan for addressing online threats.
4. **Training and Awareness**:
    - Provide regular training sessions to employees on online safety best practices.
    - Promote awareness of phishing, malware, and other common online threats.
5. **Compliance**:
    - Ensure adherence to relevant legal and regulatory requirements.
    - Monitor compliance with this policy through periodic audits.
