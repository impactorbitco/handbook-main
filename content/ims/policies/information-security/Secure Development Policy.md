## Purpose

The purpose of this Secure Development Policy is to establish a framework for ensuring that all software and systems developed and deployed within the organization adhere to robust security standards. The policy aligns with the ISO/IEC 27001 framework for Information Security Management Systems to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of organizational assets.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors involved in the development, deployment, and maintenance of software and systems within the organization. It encompasses all stages of the software development lifecycle (SDLC), including planning, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance.

## Policy Statement

The organization is committed to integrating security practices into all aspects of software and system development to mitigate risks, protect sensitive information, and ensure compliance with relevant standards and regulations. This policy is governed by the principles outlined in ISO/IEC 27001 and aims to uphold best practices in secure development.

## Objectives

1. Ensure all development activities adhere to the organization's information security policies and standards.
2. Identify and mitigate security risks throughout the SDLC.
3. Maintain compliance with ISO/IEC 27001: Information Security Management System and other applicable standards and regulations.
4. Promote a culture of secure coding and development practices across the organization.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Policy Owner (Ryan Laird):** Responsible for maintaining, updating, and ensuring compliance with this policy.
- **Developers and Engineers:** Responsible for implementing secure coding practices and adhering to security guidelines.
- **Quality Assurance (QA) Team:** Responsible for conducting security-focused testing during the development and deployment phases.
- **Management:** Responsible for providing resources and support to ensure the successful implementation of this policy.

## Key Principles

1. **Secure Coding Practices:** All developers must follow secure coding guidelines to minimize vulnerabilities and risks.
2. **Threat Modeling:** Threat modeling must be conducted during the design phase to identify potential risks and vulnerabilities.
3. **Vulnerability Management:** Identified vulnerabilities must be documented, prioritized, and remediated in a timely manner.
4. **Access Control:** Access to development environments and tools must be restricted to authorized personnel only.
5. **Testing and Validation:** Regular security testing, including penetration testing and code reviews, must be conducted to identify and address potential vulnerabilities.
6. **Documentation:** All security measures and changes must be documented to ensure traceability and accountability.

## Implementation

The organization will:

- Conduct regular training sessions for developers and engineers on secure development practices.
- Utilize tools and technologies to automate vulnerability detection and remediation.
- Establish a process for reporting and addressing security incidents related to development.
