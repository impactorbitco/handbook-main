# Information Systems Acquisition, Development, and Maintenance Policy

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines and controls for the acquisition, development, and maintenance of information systems, ensuring alignment with the organization's strategic direction and compliance with ISO/IEC 27001 standards for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS).

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all information systems managed or owned by the organization, including systems under development, those being acquired, and those undergoing maintenance. It is applicable to all employees, contractors, and third parties involved in these processes.

## 3. Policy Statement

The organization is committed to ensuring that all information systems:

- Are acquired and developed in alignment with business objectives and security requirements.
- Meet the controls and guidelines outlined in ISO/IEC 27001, Annex A7 and Annex A8 Control Groups.
- Are maintained to ensure continued compliance with security and operational standards.

## 4. Responsibilities

- **Owner:** Ryan Laird is responsible for overseeing the implementation and adherence to this policy.
- **Employees and Contractors:** All personnel are required to comply with the principles and procedures outlined in this policy.
- **Last Edited By:** Ryan Laird (May 1, 2025, 11:20 PM).

## 5. Key Guidelines

1. **Acquisition:**
    - All new systems must be evaluated for alignment with business and security requirements before acquisition.
    - Vendor and supplier assessments must be conducted to ensure compliance with relevant security standards.
2. **Development:**
    - All system development must follow secure coding practices and undergo thorough testing.
    - Risk assessments must be performed during the development lifecycle to identify and mitigate vulnerabilities.
3. **Maintenance:**
    - Regular updates and patches must be applied to all systems to address security vulnerabilities.
    - Systems must undergo periodic reviews to ensure continued alignment with organizational and regulatory requirements.
