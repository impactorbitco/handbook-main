# Secure Disposal of Equipment Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to ensure the secure and responsible disposal of equipment that may contain sensitive or confidential information. This policy aligns with the organization's commitment to information security and compliance with ISO/IEC 27001 requirements.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who handle, manage, or are responsible for the disposal of company-owned equipment, including but not limited to computers, servers, mobile devices, removable storage media, and any other hardware that may store sensitive data.

## Policy Statement

The secure disposal of equipment is critical to maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets. All equipment identified for disposal must undergo a secure and verifiable process to prevent unauthorized access to sensitive data.

## Responsibilities

- **Owner**: Ryan Laird, as the designated owner of this policy, is responsible for overseeing its implementation and ensuring compliance with organizational and ISO/IEC 27001 standards.
- **Employees and Contractors**: All personnel must adhere to this policy and report any concerns or non-compliance to their supervisor or the information security team.

## Disposal Process

1. **Identification**: Equipment due for disposal must be identified and recorded in the equipment inventory.
2. **Data Erasure**: All data stored on the equipment must be securely erased using approved data destruction tools or methods.
3. **Verification**: Verification of data erasure must be conducted and documented.
4. **Physical Destruction**: If data erasure is not feasible, equipment must be physically destroyed by an authorized vendor or through approved methods to ensure data cannot be retrieved.
5. **Documentation**: Records of disposal, including the method used and verification of destruction, must be maintained in accordance with the organization’s record-keeping policies.

## Compliance and Standards

This policy complies with the following standards:

- **ISO/IEC 27001: Information Security Management System**
- **ISO Group**: A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security

## Exceptions

Any exceptions to this policy must be approved in writing by the policy owner, Ryan Laird, and documented for audit purposes.

## Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract, and may be reported to relevant regulatory authorities where applicable.
