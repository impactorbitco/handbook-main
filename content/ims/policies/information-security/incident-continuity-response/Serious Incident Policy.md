## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to outline the organization's approach to managing and responding to serious incidents. It provides a framework for ensuring that incidents are handled effectively, minimizing their impact on employees, operations, and stakeholders, while complying with relevant standards such as ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety Management.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and stakeholders involved in organizational operations. It is designed to address incidents with potential or actual severe consequences, including but not limited to workplace injuries, data breaches, and other critical events affecting health, safety, and security.

## Policy Statement

The organization is committed to promoting a safe and secure work environment. In the event of a serious incident, the organization will ensure a prompt, structured, and effective response in accordance with its Incident Response Plans and the principles outlined in ISO 45001 and ISO Group A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security.

## Responsibilities

### Incident Management Team (IMT)

- Develop, maintain, and periodically review the organization's Incident Response Plans.
- Coordinate responses to serious incidents to ensure swift resolution and mitigation of impacts.

### Management Functions: Human Resources

- Provide support to employees affected by serious incidents.
- Ensure compliance with this policy and related legal, regulatory, and organizational requirements.

### Employees

- Report serious incidents promptly to their supervisors or the Incident Management Team.
- Follow the procedures outlined in the Incident Response Plans for their safety and the safety of others.

## Procedures

1. **Incident Reporting**

    All serious incidents must be reported immediately to the designated Incident Management Team via the appropriate channels outlined in the Incident Response Plans.

2. **Incident Assessment**

    The Incident Management Team will assess the severity of the incident based on the potential or actual impact on health, safety, operations, and information security.

3. **Response Activation**

    The relevant procedures outlined in the Incident Response Plans will be activated, including notifying affected stakeholders, securing impacted areas, and mitigating risks.

4. **Documentation and Review**

    All incidents will be documented in detail, including a summary of the event, actions taken, and lessons learned. The documentation will be reviewed to improve future incident response.

## Compliance and Verification

This policy aligns with ISO 45001 standards for Occupational Health and Safety Management and ISO Group A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security. The policy and its associated procedures are verified and maintained by Ryan Laird, the policy owner, to ensure ongoing compliance and effectiveness.
