# Portable Appliance Testing Policy

## Purpose

This Portable Appliance Testing (PAT) Policy outlines the company’s commitment to ensuring the safety of all portable electrical appliances used within our premises. This policy aligns with our strategic direction and adheres to applicable standards, including ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety Management.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and visitors who use or come into contact with portable electrical appliances on company premises. It establishes the requirements for regular testing, maintenance, and safe use of these appliances to mitigate risks and ensure compliance with health and safety regulations.

## Responsibilities

### Management Functions

The Human Resources department is responsible for overseeing the implementation and maintenance of this policy. This includes ensuring that all relevant employees are trained in the safe use of portable appliances and that PAT is conducted regularly.

### Policy Owner

Ryan Laird, as the policy owner, is responsible for ensuring the policy remains up to date and compliant with applicable standards and regulations. Any updates or revisions to this policy must be verified and approved before implementation.

## Standards and Compliance

This policy complies with:

- ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety Management
- ISO Group A.5.1: Policies for Information Security

Regular verification and audits will be conducted to ensure adherence to these standards.

## Key Principles

1. **Regular Testing**
Portable appliances must undergo testing at regular intervals as determined by risk assessments and legal requirements.
2. **Record Keeping**
Detailed records of all PAT activities, including test results, maintenance schedules, and non-compliance issues, must be maintained.
3. **Employee Training**
Employees must be trained in the proper use of portable appliances and made aware of the importance of reporting faulty equipment.
4. **Fault Reporting**
Any portable appliance showing signs of damage or malfunction must be immediately reported and removed from service until repaired or replaced.
5. **Risk Assessment**
Risk assessments must be conducted to determine the frequency of testing required for different types of portable appliances.

## Implementation and Monitoring

The policy will be implemented by Human Resources in collaboration with relevant departments. The implementation process will include:

- Identifying all portable appliances used within the company
- Establishing a schedule for regular testing
- Training staff on PAT procedures and safe appliance use

The effectiveness of this policy will be monitored through periodic audits and feedback from employees. Any incidents involving portable appliances will be thoroughly investigated to ensure continuous improvement.
