# Cloud Computing Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this Cloud Computing Policy is to establish a framework for utilizing cloud computing resources securely and effectively within the organization. This policy aligns with the information security management principles outlined in ISO/IEC 27001 and supports the organization's strategic direction.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who access or manage the organization's cloud computing resources. It covers the use of cloud-based applications, storage, and services to ensure compliance with information security standards and protect sensitive data.

## Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:

1. Ensure the secure and efficient use of cloud computing resources.
2. Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information stored in or processed through cloud services.
3. Establish clear guidelines for selecting, managing, and monitoring cloud service providers.
4. Comply with ISO/IEC 27001 standards and other relevant regulations.

## Policy Guidelines

### 1. Selection of Cloud Service Providers

- All cloud service providers must be vetted and approved by the Security Management Function.
- Providers must demonstrate compliance with ISO/IEC 27001 or equivalent standards.
- Contracts with cloud service providers should include provisions for data protection, incident reporting, and termination procedures.

### 2. Access Control

- Access to cloud resources must be restricted based on job roles and responsibilities.
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) must be implemented for all accounts accessing cloud services.
- Regular audits of access permissions must be conducted to ensure compliance.

### 3. Data Security

- Sensitive data must be encrypted both in transit and at rest.
- Data classified as confidential or higher must not be stored on cloud services without explicit approval from the Security Management Function.
- Backup and disaster recovery plans must be established for critical data stored in the cloud.

### 4. Monitoring and Audit

- All cloud services must be continuously monitored for security threats and performance issues.
- Logs of user activities and system events must be maintained and reviewed regularly.
- Annual audits of cloud service providers and usage must be conducted to ensure ongoing compliance.

### 5. Incident Management

- All incidents involving cloud resources must be reported immediately to the Security Management Function.
- An incident response plan must be in place to address potential breaches or system failures.
- Post-incident reviews must be conducted to identify root causes and implement preventive measures.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Owner (Ryan Laird):** Responsible for overseeing the implementation and compliance of this policy.
- **Security Management Function:** Responsible for vetting cloud service providers, monitoring usage, and managing incidents.
- **Employees and Contractors:** Responsible for adhering to this policy and reporting any security concerns.

## Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, termination of access to cloud resources, or other consequences as deemed appropriate by management. Compliance with this policy will be verified through regular audits and reviews.

# Policy on the Use of Cloud Services

## Introduction

This document serves as the official policy for the use of cloud services within the organization. It has been developed to ensure the secure, efficient, and compliant use of cloud technologies in alignment with the company's strategic direction and information security management standards.

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish guidelines and requirements for the adoption and use of cloud services, ensuring that they meet the organization's security and operational standards. This policy aims to mitigate risks, safeguard sensitive data, and ensure compliance with ISO/IEC 27001 and other relevant standards.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who use cloud services on behalf of the organization. It covers the procurement, implementation, and management of cloud services used for storing, processing, or transmitting organizational data.

## Objectives

- Ensure the secure use of cloud services within the organization.
- Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data stored or processed in the cloud.
- Comply with ISO/IEC 27001: Information Security Management System standards and other applicable regulations.
- Define roles and responsibilities for managing cloud services.

## Policy Statements

1. **Cloud Service Selection**: All cloud services must be approved by the organizationâ€™s Information Security Management System (ISMS) team. Only services that meet the organization's security and compliance requirements will be considered.
2. **Data Classification and Protection**: Data stored or processed in the cloud must be classified according to the organization's data classification policies. Appropriate security controls must be applied based on the classification level.
3. **Access Control**: Access to cloud services must be restricted to authorized personnel only. Multi-factor authentication (MFA) must be enforced wherever possible.
4. **Compliance and Audit**: All cloud services must comply with ISO/IEC 27001 standards and be subject to regular audits to ensure continued compliance.
5. **Incident Management**: Any security incidents involving cloud services must be reported immediately to the ISMS team and handled in accordance with the organization's incident response policy.
6. **Termination of Services**: Upon termination of a cloud service, all organizational data must be securely removed from the provider's environment to prevent unauthorized access or data breaches.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Policy Owner (Ryan Laird)**: Responsible for maintaining and updating this policy.
- **ISMS Team**: Responsible for reviewing and approving cloud service providers and ensuring compliance with organizational standards.
- **Employees and Contractors**: Required to adhere to this policy and report any violations or security incidents.

## Compliance and Enforcement

Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action, including termination of access to cloud services or other appropriate measures, as determined by the organization.
