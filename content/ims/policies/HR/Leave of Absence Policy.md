**Statement and Purpose of Policy**

1. Green Orbit Digital (**we** or **us** or **our**) acknowledges the personal nature of bereavement and is committed to supporting employees in practical and reasonable ways.
2. Bereavement or compassionate leave is leave that allows an employee time off to deal with their personal distress and related practical arrangements, primarily, but not limited to, when a member of their family passes away.
3. This Policy shows the minimum leave employees are entitled to in different circumstances.
4. We further acknowledge that:
    1. not all employees may take the full leave allowance; and
    2. some employees may need additional time, depending on their relationship with the person who has died and the circumstances of the death.
5. We will take each situation on a case-by-case basis and will discuss any particular circumstances with employees individually.
6. We may amend this Policy at any time, at our absolute discretion.

# Time off Work Policy

## Purpose

This policy outlines the procedures and guidelines for employees requesting and taking time off work. It ensures compliance with ISO 45001 standards for Occupational Health and Safety Management and promotes a balanced approach to employee well-being and organizational effectiveness.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees within the organization and is governed by the Human Resources department. It is classified under strategic direction and aligns with the organization's commitment to maintaining a safe and healthy workplace.

## Policy Statement

The organization recognizes the importance of providing employees with opportunities to take time off for rest, recovery, and personal matters. Employees are encouraged to use their allocated leave to maintain work-life balance and ensure optimal performance.

## Time Off Categories

Employees may request time off under the following categories:

1. **Annual Leave** – For personal vacations and rest days.
2. **Sick Leave** – For recovery from illness or injury.
3. **Parental Leave** – For maternity, paternity, or adoption-related absences.
4. **Unpaid Leave** – For personal reasons, subject to management approval.
5. **Compassionate Leave** – For emergencies or the loss of a loved one.

## Request Process

1. Employees must submit time-off requests through the designated system or directly to their supervisor.
2. Requests should be made with sufficient notice to allow for schedule adjustments.
3. Approval is subject to workload considerations and compliance with company guidelines.

## Responsibilities

- **Employees**: Submit requests in a timely manner and plan leaves in alignment with team responsibilities.
- **Supervisors**: Review and approve requests based on operational needs.
- **Human Resources Department**: Maintain records, ensure policy compliance, and address any concerns related to time off.

## Compliance

This policy aligns with ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety Management standards, ensuring that employee well-being remains a priority. Additionally, it adheres to the principles outlined under ISO Group A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security to maintain the integrity of employee records and data.
