# Impact Orbit Creative Group  
## Expense Policy

---

## 1. Introduction

Impact Orbit Creative Group recognises that legitimate business expenses are a necessary part of delivering our work effectively. This Expense Policy sets out clear guidance on what expenses may be claimed, how they should be approved, and the process for reimbursement.

The policy is designed to ensure consistency, transparency, and accountability, while enabling employees to carry out their roles without incurring personal financial disadvantage.

---

## 2. Eligibility

This policy applies to all employees of Impact Orbit Creative Group who incur reasonable and necessary expenses wholly and exclusively in the course of their work. This includes, but is not limited to, expenses incurred when:

- Travelling on company business  
- Attending meetings, conferences, or events  
- Representing the organisation with clients, partners, or stakeholders  
- Purchasing approved goods or services on behalf of the organisation  

---

## 3. Types of Expenses Covered

### 3.1 Travel Expenses

Reasonable travel costs incurred for business purposes, including:

- Airfare, rail, and public transport  
- Taxi fares and ride-hailing services  
- Car hire and mileage (where applicable)  
- Parking charges and tolls  

Travel should be booked using the most cost-effective option appropriate to the journey.

---

### 3.2 Accommodation

- Hotel or other short-term accommodation required for business travel  
- Accommodation should be of a reasonable standard and cost, appropriate to the location and purpose of travel  

---

### 3.3 Meals and Hospitality

- Reasonable costs for meals and non-alcoholic beverages incurred during business travel  
- Client or stakeholder hospitality where there is a clear business purpose  

Alcohol may only be claimed where reasonable and appropriate, and where prior approval has been given.

---

### 3.4 Communication Expenses

Business-related communication costs, including:

- Business calls and data usage while travelling  
- Internet access required for business purposes  
- Other necessary communication services incurred on behalf of the organisation  

---

### 3.5 Miscellaneous Business Expenses

Other reasonable and necessary expenses, such as:

- Conference or event registration fees  
- Professional subscriptions (where approved)  
- Business-related supplies and materials  
- Parking, printing, and courier costs  

---

## 4. Approval Process

### 4.1 Pre-Approval

- Where possible, expenses should be approved in advance by the employeeâ€™s line manager or department lead.
- Pre-approval may be given via email or through an approved expense management system.

---

### 4.2 Documentation

- Original, itemised receipts or invoices must be retained for all expenses.
- Proof of payment must be provided where applicable.
- Expenses without appropriate documentation may be declined.

---

### 4.3 Expense Reports

- Expense claims must be submitted within **30 days** of the expense being incurred, unless otherwise agreed.
- Expense reports must include:
  - Date of expense  
  - Amount and currency  
  - Business purpose  
  - Supporting documentation  

---

### 4.4 Approval

- All expense claims must be reviewed and approved by the relevant line manager or department lead before reimbursement is processed.

---

## 5. Reimbursement Process

### 5.1 Timely Submission

Employees are encouraged to submit expense claims promptly to support efficient processing and accurate financial reporting.

---

### 5.2 Method of Reimbursement

- Approved expenses will be reimbursed via payroll or direct bank transfer, in line with company processes.

---

### 5.3 Foreign Currency Expenses

- Expenses incurred in foreign currencies will be reimbursed using the exchange rate applicable on the date of the expense.
- Employees should provide evidence of the exchange rate used where possible.

---

### 5.4 Tax Compliance

- Expense reimbursements will be processed in accordance with HMRC guidance.
- Employees are responsible for seeking advice on any personal tax implications where required.

---

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Policy Adherence

All employees are expected to comply with this policy and exercise good judgement when incurring business expenses.

---

### 6.2 Audits and Reviews

Impact Orbit Creative Group reserves the right to review or audit expense claims to ensure accuracy and compliance with this policy.

---

### 6.3 Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- Rejection of expense claims  
- Requirement to repay incorrectly claimed amounts  
- Disciplinary action, where appropriate  

---

## 7. Conclusion

This Expense Policy supports responsible financial management while ensuring employees are reimbursed fairly for legitimate business costs. By following this policy, Impact Orbit Creative Group maintains transparency, accountability, and compliance with legal and organisational standards.
