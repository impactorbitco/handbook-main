## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to outline the organization’s commitment to preventing radicalization, promoting ethical behavior, and ensuring alignment with the principles of social responsibility and information security. This policy is developed as part of the organization’s strategic direction and commitment to fostering a safe and inclusive environment.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and stakeholders of the organization. It is designed to align with the principles set forth in ISO 26000: Social Responsibility Management System and the ISO A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security standards.

## Policy Statement

The organization is committed to promoting ethical behavior and preventing all forms of radicalization. Radicalization poses risks not only to individuals but also to the organization’s culture, reputation, and overall security. As part of our strategic direction, we aim to implement measures to identify, prevent, and respond to any signs of radicalization within the organization.

## Responsibilities

- **Management Functions**: The Ethics team, led by Ryan Laird, will oversee the implementation and monitoring of this policy.
- **Employees**: All employees have a responsibility to report any behavior or activities that may indicate radicalization.
- **Leadership**: Leadership will ensure that adequate resources are allocated to promote awareness and training on anti-radicalization measures.

## Key Principles

1. **Education and Awareness**: Regular training sessions will be provided to ensure that employees understand the risks of radicalization and the importance of maintaining a secure and ethical workplace.
2. **Monitoring and Reporting**: Mechanisms will be established to monitor and report any suspicious activities while respecting the privacy and rights of employees.
3. **Collaboration**: The organization will collaborate with relevant stakeholders to align its anti-radicalization efforts with broader social responsibility initiatives.
4. **Continuous Improvement**: The policy will be reviewed periodically to ensure its effectiveness and alignment with the latest standards and practices.

## Governance

This policy is classified under the organization’s **Level 1 – Strategic Direction** hierarchy and is assigned the ID: **961**. It is part of the SRMS (Social Responsibility Management System) and adheres to the standards of ISO 26000 and ISO A.5.1.
