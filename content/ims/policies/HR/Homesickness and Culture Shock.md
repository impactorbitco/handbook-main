## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to provide guidance and support to employees experiencing homesickness and culture shock, ensuring their well-being and productivity while adapting to a new environment. This document aligns with ISO 45001 standards for Occupational Health and Safety Management and reflects the organization's commitment to employee mental health and cultural integration.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees of the organization, particularly those who have recently relocated for work or are engaging with new cultural environments. It is designed to address challenges related to homesickness and culture shock, offering practical solutions and resources.

## Policy Statement

The organization recognizes that homesickness and culture shock can significantly impact an individual's mental health, job performance, and overall well-being. As part of our commitment to occupational health and safety, we strive to create a supportive environment where employees feel comfortable seeking help and accessing resources to navigate these challenges.

## Objectives

1. To provide resources and tools that help employees manage homesickness and culture shock effectively.
2. To foster a supportive workplace culture that acknowledges and respects cultural differences.
3. To ensure compliance with ISO 45001 standards related to occupational health and safety.

## Responsibilities

### Management Functions

- The Human Resources (HR) department, under the leadership of Ryan Laird, is responsible for implementing and maintaining this policy.
- HR will provide training to managers on recognizing signs of homesickness and culture shock in employees and addressing them appropriately.
- HR will ensure the availability of resources, such as counseling services and cultural training sessions.

### Employees

- Employees are encouraged to communicate openly with their managers or HR representatives about challenges they may face related to homesickness or culture shock.
- Employees are expected to actively participate in any programs or resources provided to aid their adjustment.

## Resources and Support

The organization offers the following support mechanisms:

- Access to counseling services for employees experiencing emotional distress.
- Cultural training programs to provide insights into the local culture and practices.
- Peer support groups to foster a sense of community among employees.

## Monitoring and Review

This policy will be reviewed periodically by the Human Resources department to ensure effectiveness and alignment with ISO 45001 standards. Feedback from employees will be actively sought to improve support mechanisms.

## Responsibilities for Implementation

Ryan Laird, as the policy owner, will oversee the implementation and monitoring of this policy. The Human Resources department will ensure that all employees are informed of the policy and have access to the necessary resources.
