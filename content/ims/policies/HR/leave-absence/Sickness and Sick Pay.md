## Purpose

This policy outlines the approach and guidelines for managing sickness and sick pay within the organization. It is designed to ensure fair treatment of employees, promote health and well-being, and comply with relevant occupational health and safety standards.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees of the organization. It is aligned with ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety Management standards and supports the organization’s commitment to Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 01 - No Poverty.

## Policy Statement

The organization is committed to supporting employees who are unable to work due to illness or injury. This includes providing clear guidelines on sick pay entitlements and fostering a culture of understanding and care.

## Key Principles

1. **Fairness and Equity**: All employees will be treated fairly and consistently in accordance with this policy.
2. **Compliance with Standards**: The policy aligns with ISO 45001 standards and supports health and safety management within the workplace.
3. **Support for Well-being**: The organization prioritizes the health and well-being of employees by providing appropriate support during periods of illness.

## Sick Pay Entitlements

- Employees are entitled to sick pay as outlined in their employment contracts or relevant collective agreements.
- Sick pay will be calculated based on the employee's regular pay and will comply with applicable legal and regulatory requirements.

## Reporting Sickness

- Employees must notify their line manager or HR department as soon as possible if they are unable to attend work due to illness.
- A medical certificate may be required for absences exceeding a specified duration, as outlined in the employee handbook.

## Return to Work

- The organization will support employees returning to work after a period of illness through a structured return-to-work process.
- Adjustments to duties or working conditions may be made to accommodate employees recovering from illness or injury.

## Responsibilities

- **Employees**: Notify management promptly of any illness or injury affecting their ability to work and provide necessary documentation when required.
- **Management**: Ensure employees are aware of their rights and responsibilities under this policy and provide support as needed.
- **Human Resources**: Maintain records of sickness absences and ensure compliance with legal and organizational requirements.
