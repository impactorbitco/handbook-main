## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to provide a strategic direction for managing workplace stress in alignment with ISO 45001 standards for Occupational Health and Safety Management. The policy underscores our commitment to promoting employee health and safety by identifying, addressing, and mitigating stress-related risks in the workplace.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and other stakeholders operating within the organization. It is part of our overarching Health & Safety Management framework and is classified as a Level 1 â€“ Strategic Direction policy under IMS reference OHSM/A5.1/POL/1039.

## Policy Statement

The organization recognizes that workplace stress can negatively impact employee health, safety, and overall productivity. To address this, we are committed to:

- Identifying potential stressors in the workplace.
- Implementing measures to minimize and manage workplace stress.
- Providing support and resources to employees experiencing work-related stress.
- Ensuring compliance with ISO 45001 standards to maintain a safe and healthy work environment.

## Key Elements

1. **Work Hours Management**: In line with our statement on work hours, we will ensure that employees are not overburdened by excessive workloads or unreasonable deadlines.
2. **Health & Safety Integration**: This policy forms part of our health and safety management functions and aligns with ISO Group A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security to ensure a holistic approach to workplace well-being.
3. **Training and Education**: Employees and managers will receive training on recognizing and managing stress effectively.
4. **Support Mechanisms**: Access to counseling services and stress management programs will be provided as needed.
5. **Monitoring and Review**: Stress management initiatives and their effectiveness will be regularly monitored and reviewed by relevant stakeholders.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Management**: Responsible for implementing and maintaining this policy, ensuring that stressors are identified and mitigated.
- **Employees**: Expected to report stressors and participate in stress management initiatives.
- **Policy Owner**: Ryan Laird, as the policy owner, is responsible for overseeing its implementation and ensuring compliance with standards.
