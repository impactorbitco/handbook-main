## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish the framework and guidelines for the management, engagement, and monitoring of temporary and agency workers within the organization. This policy aligns with the organization’s strategic direction and complies with ISO 37301: Compliance Management System and A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security standards.

## Scope

This policy applies to all temporary and agency workers engaged by the organization. It includes individuals contracted through third-party agencies, as well as any short-term or non-permanent roles directly hired by the organization. The policy ensures compliance with Human Resources (HR) management functions and the organization’s overarching information security and compliance frameworks.

## Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:

1. Ensure all temporary and agency workers are fully integrated into the organization’s operational and compliance requirements.
2. Protect the organization’s information security by establishing clear expectations and protocols for temporary and agency workers.
3. Provide fair and transparent processes for the engagement, monitoring, and termination of temporary and agency workers.
4. Align the management of temporary and agency workers with the strategic goals of the organization and ISO 37301 standards.

## Key Principles

1. **Onboarding and Training**

    All temporary and agency workers must undergo a standardized onboarding process. This includes an introduction to the organization’s policies, procedures, and standards for information security and compliance.

2. **Access Control**

    Temporary and agency workers will be granted access to organizational resources only to the extent necessary to fulfill their job responsibilities. Access will be regularly reviewed and revoked upon completion of their contract.

3. **Compliance Requirements**

    Temporary and agency workers must adhere to all organizational policies, including information security measures outlined in A.5.1 - Policies for Information Security. Any breach of these requirements will result in immediate review and possible termination of the worker’s engagement.

4. **Monitoring and Evaluation**

    The performance and compliance of temporary and agency workers will be monitored by their respective supervisors. Regular evaluations will ensure adherence to organizational standards and alignment with strategic objectives.

5. **Termination of Engagement**

    The engagement of temporary and agency workers will be terminated in accordance with the terms of their contract. Proper offboarding procedures will be followed to ensure the protection of organizational assets and information.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Human Resources (HR):** Responsible for overseeing the engagement process, including onboarding, contract management, and compliance monitoring.
- **Supervisors/Managers:** Accountable for the day-to-day management of temporary and agency workers, including performance monitoring and compliance with information security policies.
- **Temporary and Agency Workers:** Expected to comply with all organizational policies and complete their responsibilities in line with the organization’s standards.

## Governance and Review

This policy is owned by **Ryan Laird**, with oversight provided by the Human Resources (HR) management function. The policy will be reviewed periodically to ensure continued alignment with organizational priorities, ISO 37301 standards, and A.5.1 information security policies.
