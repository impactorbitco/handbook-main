## Introduction

Impact Orbit Creative Group recognises that corporate social responsibility (CSR) is multi-dimensional and should reflect economic, legal, ethical, and philanthropic responsibilities. This policy aligns our sustainability, social, and governance initiatives with Carroll’s CSR Pyramid to ensure a balanced, strategic, and actionable approach.

## Purpose

- Provide a structured framework for CSR aligned with Carroll’s four layers.  
- Demonstrate our commitment to ethical business practices and sustainable impact.  
- Guide employees, management, and partners in contributing to responsible corporate conduct.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, suppliers, partners, and stakeholders involved in the organization’s operations or activities.

---

## CSR Commitments by Carroll’s Pyramid

### 1. **Economic Responsibilities**
We acknowledge that the foundation of CSR is economic performance. We are committed to:

- Operating profitably and efficiently to ensure business sustainability.  
- Delivering value to shareholders, customers, and employees.  
- Supporting local economic development by hiring locally and sourcing from local suppliers where possible.  
- Maintaining financial integrity and transparency in all operations.

### 2. **Legal Responsibilities**
Compliance with laws and regulations is the second layer of our CSR approach:

- Adhering to all local, national, and international laws governing business conduct.  
- Complying with labour laws, environmental regulations, anti-money laundering legislation, and industry standards.  
- Ensuring suppliers and contractors meet legal obligations, including anti-slavery, child labour, and human rights standards.  
- Maintaining clear reporting and audit mechanisms for legal compliance.

### 3. **Ethical Responsibilities**
Beyond economic and legal duties, we pursue ethical responsibilities to meet societal expectations:

- Acting with integrity, fairness, and transparency in all interactions.  
- Minimizing environmental impact through sustainable practices, energy efficiency, and waste reduction.  
- Respecting human rights, promoting diversity, equality, and inclusion across all operations.  
- Encouraging responsible innovation, ethical supply chain management, and stakeholder engagement.  

### 4. **Philanthropic Responsibilities**
At the top of Carroll’s Pyramid, we actively contribute to the well-being of communities and society:

- Supporting educational, environmental, and social programs, including the Youth Environmental Programme and Bright Green Future initiatives.  
- Promoting volunteerism, employee-led community engagement, and partnerships with NGOs.  
- Providing pro bono services and other resources to support local communities.  
- Funding initiatives that address social challenges, foster innovation, and enhance quality of life.

## Responsibilities

- **Policy Owner**: Ryan Laird is responsible for ensuring the CSR policy is implemented, monitored, and updated.  
- **Sustainability Team**: Coordinates CSR programs, monitors compliance, and reports on progress.  
- **All Employees and Partners**: Expected to follow the CSR principles, actively participate in initiatives, and report concerns or opportunities.  

---

## Implementation and Monitoring

- CSR activities will be planned, executed, and measured according to Carroll’s four-layer framework.  
- Regular performance reviews, stakeholder feedback, and impact assessments will guide continuous improvement.  
- Key CSR metrics will be reported internally and externally where appropriate.

---

## Review and Update

- This policy will be reviewed annually and updated to reflect regulatory changes, best practices, and organizational priorities.  
- Changes will be communicated to employees, partners, and stakeholders.
