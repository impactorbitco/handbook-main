## 1. Purpose and Scope

Impact Orbit Creative Group is committed to **continual improvement in energy performance**, the efficient and responsible use of energy, and alignment with our broader sustainability, decarbonisation, and net-zero objectives.

This policy applies to all operations under our control, including:

- Office operations and facilities management  
- Digital infrastructure, campaign delivery, and IT systems  
- Business travel, commuting, and logistics  
- Procurement, supply chain, and vendor relationships  

It encompasses all employees, contractors, consultants, suppliers, and stakeholders whose activities influence energy use or efficiency.

---

## 2. Policy Objectives

We are committed to:

- **Improving energy performance annually** to support sustainable growth and carbon neutrality.  
- Establishing, implementing, and maintaining an **Energy Management System (EnMS)** aligned with ISO 50001 standards.  
- Providing **accurate, timely, and actionable energy data** alongside tools, resources, and training to drive efficiency.  
- Embedding energy efficiency and sustainability into **operational planning, innovation, and decision-making**.  
- **Complying with all applicable legal and regulatory obligations**, including UK energy regulations and international climate commitments.  
- Procuring **energy-efficient products, services, and renewable energy solutions** wherever feasible.  
- **Communicating, reviewing, and updating** this policy regularly to ensure relevance, accountability, and engagement across the organisation.

---

## 3. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Energy Performance** | Measurable outcomes relating to energy efficiency, use, and consumption. |
| **EnMS** | Systematic Energy Management System supporting continual improvement in energy performance. |
| **Energy Baseline** | Reference measurement for comparing future energy performance. |
| **Significant Energy Use (SEU)** | Energy-intensive activities or assets with high improvement potential. |
| **Carbon Neutrality** | Achieving net-zero carbon emissions through reduction and verified offsetting. |

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

- **Board / Executive Leadership**: Oversight, review, and strategic alignment with sustainability goals.  
- **Sustainability / Energy Officer**: Coordination, monitoring, and reporting of energy performance.  
- **Managers**: Ensure operational compliance, promote energy efficiency, and support employee engagement.  
- **All Employees**: Follow energy management procedures, contribute ideas for efficiency, and report anomalies or opportunities.

---

## 5. Energy Management Commitments

### 5.1 Continuous Improvement

- Set **SMART energy objectives and KPIs**, reviewed annually.  
- Conduct **energy audits** to identify SEUs and opportunities for improvement.  
- Benchmark performance internally and against industry best practices.  
- Maintain leadership accountability for energy performance outcomes.

### 5.2 Information, Tools, and Resources

- Provide staff with **training, awareness materials, and performance dashboards**.  
- Deploy **monitoring systems** to track consumption and identify trends.  
- Allocate budget and personnel for energy optimisation projects.

### 5.3 Compliance and Legal Obligations

- Ensure compliance with UK ESOS, SECR, ISO 50001, and other relevant legislation.  
- Stay aligned with evolving regulatory and sector-specific energy requirements.

---

## 6. Sustainable Procurement and Design

- Integrate **energy performance criteria** in procurement decisions.  
- Prioritise **low-carbon, energy-efficient, and circular economy solutions** from suppliers.  
- Incorporate **lifecycle energy assessments** in design, planning, and operational decisions.  
- Where feasible, use **renewable energy sources** to meet operational needs.

---

## 7. Employee Engagement and Communication

- Share **energy performance insights and progress** across the organisation.  
- Provide **energy literacy training** and workshops for employees and key stakeholders.  
- Include energy efficiency as part of **onboarding and induction programmes**.  
- Maintain a transparent **EnMS dashboard** showing goals, actions, and progress.

---

## 8. Monitoring, Reporting, and Review

Energy performance will be monitored using key indicators such as:

- Total and per-activity energy consumption (offices, digital campaigns, travel)  
- Progress toward energy reduction targets  
- Energy cost efficiency  
- Carbon impact from energy consumption  

Data will be **reviewed quarterly** and reported annually in the **Sustainability Report**, including recommendations for improvements.

---

## 9. Exceptions

- Any deviation must be **approved in writing** by top management.  
- Exceptions must be **time-limited** with a corrective plan.  
- All exceptions require **documentation and risk assessment**.
