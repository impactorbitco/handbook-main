# Security and Transmission of Personal Confidential Data

## Introduction

This policy outlines the strategic direction and guidelines for the security and transmission of personal confidential data within the organization. It aims to ensure compliance with international standards such as ISO/IEC 27001 and to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of sensitive information. The document is classified as a Level 1 – Strategic Direction policy and is in alignment with the organization’s commitment to information security, cybersecurity, and privacy protection.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who have access to personal confidential data. It governs the handling, storage, and transmission of such data across all organizational systems, processes, and platforms.

---

## Objectives

The objectives of this policy are as follows:

1. **Ensure Information Security Compliance:** Adhere to ISO/IEC 27001 standards and other relevant regulations to maintain effective information security management systems (ISMS).
2. **Maintain Data Confidentiality:** Protect sensitive personal data from unauthorized access or disclosure.
3. **Support Cybersecurity Measures:** Implement robust cybersecurity protocols to safeguard data during transmission.
4. **Protect Intellectual Property (IP):** Preserve trade secrets, proprietary knowledge, and other intellectual property from risks.
5. **Foster a Culture of Security:** Enhance awareness and accountability for data security among employees and stakeholders.

---

## Key Principles

### 1. **Data Classification**

- All personal confidential data must be classified and labeled according to its sensitivity level.
- Data classification guidelines are detailed in the related document: [Data Handling Guide].

### 2. **Data Access Control**

- Access to personal confidential data must be restricted based on roles and responsibilities.
- Employees must use secure authentication methods, such as passwords generated through tools like [1Password Support].

### 3. **Data Transmission**

- All data transmissions must be encrypted using secure communication protocols, such as HTTPS or VPNs.
- Privacy cards or secure channels must be used when transmitting sensitive information.

### 4. **Data Storage**

- Personal confidential data must be stored securely, with appropriate encryption mechanisms in place.
- Web accounts and digital platforms used for storage must comply with organizational security policies.

### 5. **Incident Response**

- Any security breaches or incidents involving personal confidential data must be reported immediately to the Security Management team.
- Incident response protocols are outlined in the organizational [Security Policy (Internal)].
