title: Service Designer
---

At GitLab, Service Design is a discipline within User Experience Research (UXR). Service Designers collaborate with Product teams and stakeholders to design and optimise end‑to‑end experiences across products, ensuring users successfully achieve their goals throughout their lifecycle.

## Basic Responsibilities

- **Design and optimise user journeys across products** to ensure consistent, seamless experiences  
- **Partner with product, engineering, and business teams** to implement service design solutions, facilitate workshops, and co‑create sessions  
- **Conduct research and use insights, stakeholder feedback, and data** to inform and validate design decisions  
- **Create and test service prototypes**, refining them based on user feedback  
- **Help teams adopt and implement new service solutions** through clear roadmaps and frameworks  
- **Contribute to a collaborative, open team culture** by participating in peer reviews, sharing knowledge in team discussions, and mentoring others  

## Basic Requirements

- Ability to use GitLab  
- **Systems Thinking**: connect various touchpoints and service components  
- **Design Communication**: convey information using the most effective medium for the audience (e.g. service blueprints, flow diagrams)  
- **Strategic Design**: align service solutions with business capabilities and constraints  
- **Collaborative Design and Implementation**: work across teams, design for feasibility, organisational alignment, and sustainability  
- **Product & Business Acumen**: understand business goals, user needs, and technical constraints  
- **Change Management & Implementation**: ensure design solutions are adopted and scalable  
- **UX Research & Design Thinking**: leverage qualitative research to inform service innovations  

## Levels

### Service Designer (Intermediate)

Reports to a UX Research Manager.  

**Job Grade**: grade 6  

**Responsibilities & Requirements**  
(as above under Basic Responsibilities / Requirements)  

### Senior Service Designer  

Reports to a UX Research Manager.  

**Job Grade**: grade 7  

**Additional Responsibilities**  
- Design and enhance end‑to‑end user experiences across complex user journeys  
- Develop and implement service design frameworks covering full user lifecycle, personas, and segments  
- Collaborate deeply with product, engineering, and business teams to drive execution  
- Use research, stakeholder input, and operational data to validate and refine service improvements  
- Prototype, test, and iterate service concepts to enhance interactions and improve service effectiveness  
- Lead strategic service design initiatives, driving innovation and system‑wide optimisations  
- Establish scalable, repeatable service design methodologies and maintain consistency across the organisation  
- Mentor UX team members on complex design challenges and strategic thinking  

**Requirements**  
- Experienced in designing complex service ecosystems across products  
- Strong user‑centric design thinking, persona frameworks, and lifecycle strategy skills  
- Excellent visualization and storytelling using tools like Figma, Miro, service blueprints, and mockups  
- Strong stakeholder engagement and ability to lead cross‑functional workshops  
- Solid product, business, and technical acumen to balance user needs, business priorities, and operational constraints  
- Experience in change management and scaling service changes across teams  
- Proficiency in user‑research methods including surveys, interviews, and usability testing  
- Leadership capabilities: influence decisions, champion service design practices, and foster a culture of learning  
- Coaching & mentoring skills to support growth of UX professionals and design maturity  

### Staff Service Designer  

Leads service design initiatives with cross‑functional impact.  

**Job Grade**: grade 8  

**Responsibilities & Requirements**  
- Lead strategic service design initiatives affecting multiple teams or products  
- Refine and scale service design methodologies for sustainability and consistency across the organisation  
- Influence business and technology strategy based on service design insights and user‑centered research  
- Mentor senior and junior design staff, guiding on high‑complexity design problems and systemic improvements  
- Drive organizational alignment for service‑level initiatives, ensuring adoption and scale  

## Hiring Process

Candidates for this position can expect the following hiring stages (subject to change):

1. **Recruiter Screening Call** — 30 min to discuss background, expectations, and motivations for applying  
2. **Portfolio Review & Interview** — candidates submit portfolio; if strong, follow with a 90‑minute interview with hiring manager and UX researcher, often including a case study walkthrough  
3. **Team Interviews** — meetings with Product Design Manager (focus: research, strategy, service design skills) and with Product & Engineering leads (focus: collaboration and execution fit)  
4. **Offer Stage** — following successful interviews, offer extended via video or phone  

Additional details and variations may be found on the hiring page.
