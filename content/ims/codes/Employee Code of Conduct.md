**Applies to:** All employees, contractors, volunteers, and individuals representing Impact Orbit Creative Group, including Green Orbit Digital, Green Orbit Space Communications & PR, and Green Orbit Academy.  

This Employee Code of Conduct outlines the principles, ethics, and behaviours expected of all personnel to maintain a safe, respectful, and productive workplace. It also supports compliance with legal and regulatory requirements, promotes safeguarding, and reinforces our organisational values.

---

## 1. Purpose
Impact Orbit Creative Group is committed to:  
- Fostering an inclusive, safe, and harassment-free environment  
- Promoting respect, integrity, and professional behaviour at all times  
- Upholding sustainability, social responsibility, and ethical business practices  

All employees are expected to model these values in their daily work and interactions, whether in-person, online, or representing the organisation externally.

---

## 2. Core Principles

### 2.1 Respect and Inclusivity
- Treat colleagues, partners, participants, and suppliers with dignity and courtesy.  
- Respect diversity across race, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, ethnicity, nationality, religion, disability, age, socioeconomic background, and beliefs.  
- Avoid harassment, bullying, or any behaviour that could create a hostile or intimidating environment.  
- Listen actively, acknowledge different perspectives, and encourage collaboration.  

### 2.2 Professional Conduct
- Act with honesty, integrity, and transparency in all dealings.  
- Maintain confidentiality and handle sensitive information securely.  
- Avoid conflicts of interest and disclose any potential conflicts to management.  
- Use organisational resources responsibly, including IT systems, property, and branding.  

### 2.3 Health, Safety, and Wellbeing
- Follow health, safety, and safeguarding policies to protect yourself and others.  
- Report unsafe conditions or behaviour immediately.  
- Take care of your own wellbeing and respect the boundaries of others.  

### 2.4 Ethical Business Practices
- Comply with anti-bribery, anti-corruption, and anti-money laundering regulations.  
- Avoid offering or accepting inappropriate gifts, entertainment, or incentives.  
- Ensure fair treatment of customers, suppliers, and partners in accordance with contractual and legal obligations.  
- Protect the organisation’s reputation and avoid actions that could bring it into disrepute.  

### 2.5 Environmental and Social Responsibility
- Act in alignment with Impact Orbit’s sustainability and environmental commitments.  
- Support inclusive, socially responsible, and ethical practices internally and across our supply chains.  

---

## 3. Expectations of Employee Behaviour

Employees must:  
- Observe the terms of their employment contract, company policies, and procedures.  
- Comply with all legal and regulatory requirements in the jurisdictions where we operate.  
- Take reasonable care for health, safety, and wellbeing of themselves and colleagues.  
- Conduct interactions with colleagues, customers, suppliers, and partners fairly, honestly, and respectfully.  
- Act in good faith, in the organisation’s best interests, and in alignment with our values.  

---

## 4. Misconduct

### 4.1 General
Actions or behaviours outside generally accepted standards may lead to disciplinary action, examples include:  
- Minor breaches of company policies or employment contract  
- Poor timekeeping, unauthorized absence, or leaving the workplace without permission  
- Disrespectful or uncooperative behaviour  
- Negligence in performing duties  
- Minor health and safety violations  
- Misuse of IT, social media, or company resources  

### 4.2 Gross Misconduct
Serious actions that prejudice the organisation or its reputation may lead to summary dismissal, examples include:  
- Theft, fraud, or falsification of records  
- Bribery or corruption  
- Violence, intimidation, or harassment  
- Serious breach of confidentiality or data protection  
- Deliberate damage to company property or IT systems  
- Serious health and safety violations  
- Criminal offences affecting job performance or trust  

---

## 5. Reporting and Investigating Breaches
- Employees have a duty to report suspected breaches of this Code to their manager, HR, or a safeguarding contact.  
- All reports will be investigated fairly and confidentially.  
- Breaches may result in disciplinary action, up to and including dismissal, depending on severity.  
- Protection from retaliation is guaranteed for employees reporting concerns in good faith.  

---

## 6. Compliance and Training
- Employees must complete mandatory compliance, safeguarding, and ethics training.  
- Line managers are responsible for reinforcing the Code of Conduct and addressing concerns.  
- The organisation reserves the right to revise this Code to reflect legal, ethical, or business changes.
