**Applies to:** All suppliers, contractors, business partners, and subcontractors of Impact Orbit Creative Group and its brands, including Impact Orbit Creative Group, Green Orbit Space Communications & PR, and Green Orbit Academy.  

This Supplier Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for suppliers and business partners to operate ethically, legally, and sustainably. Compliance with this Code is a condition of doing business with Impact Orbit Creative Group.  

---

## 1. Introduction
Impact Orbit Creative Group is committed to fair, honest, and responsible business practices. All suppliers are expected to uphold the highest ethical and professional standards and ensure that their subcontractors do the same.  

Failure to comply with this Code may result in immediate termination of business agreements.  

---

## 2. Human Rights and Labour Principles

### 2.1 Human Rights
Suppliers must respect universal human rights and ensure:  
- No child labour or exploitation  
- No forced or compulsory labour  
- No physical punishment or abuse  
- No unlawful discrimination in employment  

### 2.2 Modern Slavery
Suppliers must have zero tolerance for slavery or human trafficking and ensure their operations and supply chains comply with modern slavery laws.  

### 2.3 Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination
Suppliers must provide a workplace free from harassment and discrimination based on:  
- Gender, gender identity, or expression  
- Sexual orientation  
- Race, ethnicity, or nationality  
- Religion or belief  
- Age or disability  
- Pregnancy, marital status, or family responsibilities  
- Any other protected characteristic under law  

### 2.4 Child Labour
No employment of children below local legal minimum age or 15 years, whichever is higher.  

### 2.5 Forced Labour
No use of forced, bonded, or prison labour in any form.  

### 2.6 Working Conditions
Suppliers must provide safe, hygienic, and healthy working conditions, including:  
- Safe and well-maintained equipment  
- Clean and safe workplaces  
- Emergency preparedness and safety measures  

### 2.7 Wages and Hours
Suppliers must ensure fair and timely payment and comply with applicable laws on working hours and overtime. Overtime should be limited wherever possible.  

### 2.8 Freedom of Association
Suppliers must respect employees' rights to form or join trade unions and bargain collectively without retaliation.  

---

## 3. Environmental Responsibility
Suppliers are expected to minimize their environmental impact by:  
- Complying with environmental laws, permits, and regulations  
- Efficient use of resources (energy, water, and materials)  
- Responsible waste management and recycling  
- Reducing greenhouse gas emissions  
- Protecting biodiversity and ecosystems  

Suppliers are encouraged to implement environmental policies or management systems and pursue continuous improvement in sustainability.  

---

## 4. Business Ethics

### 4.1 Legal Compliance
Suppliers must comply with all applicable laws and industry standards, including:  
- labour and employment laws  
- Environmental and health regulations  
- Anti-bribery, anti-corruption, and anti-fraud laws  
- Trade and sanctions regulations  

### 4.2 Anti-Bribery and Conflicts of Interest
- No bribery, corruption, or fraud  
- Full transparency in corporate records  
- Disclose any conflicts of interest with Impact Orbit Creative Group or its representatives  

### 4.3 Data Privacy and Security
Suppliers must safeguard confidential and personal information in compliance with applicable laws and agreements with Impact Orbit Creative Group.  

### 4.4 Trade Controls
Suppliers must comply with all applicable trade regulations, including export controls, sanctions, and embargoes.  

---

## 5. Quality, Health, and Safety
Suppliers must maintain high standards of quality, safety, and hygiene, including:  
- Quality management systems  
- Product safety and compliance  
- Regular audits and assessments  
- Health and safety training and protective measures  
- Emergency preparedness  

---

## 6. Corporate Responsibility and Sustainability
Suppliers should:  
- Support sustainable procurement and responsible sourcing  
- Maintain fair and timely payment practices, including subcontractors  
- Promote diversity, inclusion, and social responsibility in their operations  
- Uphold transparency, ethical conduct, and public trust  

---

## 7. Outsourced Service Providers
Suppliers acting as outsourced service providers must comply with this Code and establish effective internal controls to deliver services safely, ethically, and legally.  

---

## 8. Compliance and Reporting

### 8.1 Reporting Violations
Suppliers are encouraged to report any violations of this Code or unethical practices to [compliance@impactorbit.co](mailto:compliance@impactorbit.co).  

### 8.2 Impact Orbit Creative Group Rights
- Suppliers may be required to complete compliance self-assessments  
- Suppliers must ensure subcontractors adhere to this Code  
- Impact Orbit Creative Group may request corrective actions, suspend, or terminate relationships for non-compliance  
- Severe violations may be reported to relevant authorities  

### 8.3 Updates
Impact Orbit Creative Group reserves the right to modify this Code with notice.  

---

## 9. Supplier Acknowledgment
By doing business with Impact Orbit Creative Group, suppliers confirm that they:  
- Have read, understood, and agree to abide by this Code  
- Will communicate its requirements to employees and subcontractors  
- Will maintain appropriate documentation and continuously improve practices
