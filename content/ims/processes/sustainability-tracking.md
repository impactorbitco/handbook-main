## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines how the organisation tracks, monitors, and reports sustainability performance within the Integrated Management System (IMS). The purpose is to ensure that environmental, social, and governance (ESG) objectives are measured, controlled, and improved over time, supporting compliance, risk management, and organisational responsibility.

⸻

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to:
  • All organisational activities with environmental, social, or governance impacts
  • Personnel responsible for sustainability data collection, analysis, and reporting
  • Projects, operations, and processes across the organisation
  • External reporting obligations or internal sustainability initiatives

⸻

## 3. Principles

The organisation’s sustainability tracking is guided by:
  • Alignment with organisational ESG objectives and policies
  • Evidence-based measurement and monitoring
  • Integration with IMS processes and controls
  • Transparency and accountability in reporting
  • Continual improvement through lessons learned and stakeholder feedback

⸻

## 4. Sustainability Metrics

Sustainability performance is tracked using key indicators, including but not limited to:
  • Environmental: energy consumption, greenhouse gas emissions, waste management, water usage
  • Social: employee engagement, diversity and inclusion, community impact, health and safety metrics
  • Governance: compliance with regulations, ethical practices, risk management, policy adherence

Metrics are reviewed and updated periodically to ensure relevance and accuracy.

⸻

## 5. Data Collection and Monitoring
  • Identify data sources and responsible personnel
  • Implement tools, systems, or templates for consistent data collection
  • Schedule regular monitoring intervals (e.g., monthly, quarterly, annually)
  • Ensure data integrity, security, and accuracy

⸻

## 6. Analysis and Reporting
  • Analyse data to assess performance against sustainability objectives and targets
  • Identify trends, risks, opportunities, and areas for improvement
  • Generate reports for management, stakeholders, and regulatory requirements
  • Communicate findings internally to support awareness and engagement

⸻

## 7. Action and Improvement
  • Use analysis results to implement corrective or preventive actions
  • Update processes, policies, or initiatives to enhance sustainability performance
  • Integrate lessons learned into future sustainability planning
  • Track effectiveness of improvement actions through subsequent measurement

⸻

## 8. Roles and Responsibilities
  • Top Management: Sets sustainability objectives, reviews performance, and allocates resources
  • IMS Lead: Ensures integration of sustainability tracking within IMS processes
  • Department Heads: Collect and validate data, implement improvement actions
  • All Personnel: Contribute to sustainability initiatives and accurate reporting

⸻

## 9. Records and Documentation

Records supporting sustainability tracking include:
  • Collected data and monitoring logs
  • Analysis and performance reports
  • Improvement action plans and evidence of implementation
  • External reporting submissions and communications

All records are maintained in accordance with IMS document and records control procedures.

⸻

## 10. Continual Improvement

Sustainability tracking is part of a continual improvement cycle where:
  • Performance trends inform strategic and operational decisions
  • Lessons learned and best practices are incorporated into processes
  • Adjustments are made to targets, metrics, or controls to enhance outcomes
