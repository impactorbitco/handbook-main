## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines how the organisation undertakes strategic planning within the Integrated Management System (IMS). The purpose is to establish and maintain a structured approach to setting objectives, determining priorities, allocating resources, and ensuring alignment with organisational vision, mission, and IMS requirements.

Strategic planning ensures that the organisation’s direction is clearly defined, risks are managed, opportunities are identified, and continual improvement is embedded in organisational development.

⸻

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to:
- All strategic and operational planning activities
- Top management and relevant stakeholders responsible for organisational direction
- Planning related to quality, environmental, information security, health and safety, and other IMS aspects
- Integration of strategic objectives into operational and project-level planning

⸻

## 3. Principles

The organisation’s strategic planning is guided by the following principles:
- Alignment with organisational vision, mission, and values
- Evidence-based and risk-informed decision-making
- Integration of IMS requirements and compliance obligations
- Stakeholder engagement and consultation
- Flexibility to adapt to changes in internal or external context
- Continuous review and improvement

⸻

## 4. Strategic Planning Process

### 4.1 Context Analysis
- Review internal and external factors affecting the organisation
- Analyse strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats (SWOT)
- Identify legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements
- Assess stakeholder needs and expectations

### 4.2 Objective Setting
- Define strategic objectives aligned with organisational vision and mission
- Ensure objectives are SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Integrate objectives across IMS areas (quality, environmental, information security, health and safety, etc.)

### 4.3 Resource Allocation
- Identify resources required to achieve strategic objectives
- Allocate responsibilities and accountabilities
- Consider skills, capabilities, technology, and budget requirements

### 4.4 Risk and Opportunity Assessment
- Identify risks and opportunities associated with strategic objectives
- Evaluate impact and likelihood
- Implement risk treatment or opportunity exploitation plans

### 4.5 Action Planning
- Develop strategic initiatives and programmes
- Define timelines, milestones, and success criteria
- Ensure alignment with operational and project-level plans

### 4.6 Monitoring and Review
- Monitor progress against strategic objectives and key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Conduct regular management review to evaluate effectiveness and adjust plans
- Incorporate lessons learned and feedback into future planning cycles

⸻

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities
- Top Management: Provides direction, approves strategic plans, and reviews performance
- IMS Lead: Ensures integration of IMS requirements into strategic planning
- Department/Function Heads: Develop action plans, allocate resources, and report progress
- All Personnel: Support achievement of objectives within their areas of responsibility

⸻

## 6. Documentation and Records

Records supporting strategic planning include:
- Strategic plans and objectives
- SWOT and context analysis documentation
- Risk and opportunity assessments
- Management review minutes and reports
- Performance monitoring data

All records are controlled under IMS document and records control procedures.

⸻

## 7. Continual Improvement

Strategic planning is part of a continual improvement cycle where:
- Performance against objectives is evaluated
- Lessons learned inform future strategy
- Adjustments are made to improve effectiveness, efficiency, and alignment with organisational goals
