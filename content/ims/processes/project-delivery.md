## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines how projects are planned, executed, monitored, and closed within the organisation, using a structured approach aligned with the Project Management Professional (PMP) methodology and the Integrated Management System (IMS). The objective is to ensure consistent, controlled, and high-quality project delivery while managing risks, resources, and stakeholder expectations.

⸻

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to:
- All projects undertaken by the organisation, regardless of size or complexity
- Personnel involved in project management, execution, or support
- External partners or suppliers contributing to projects
- Projects with quality, environmental, information security, or health and safety considerations

⸻

## 3. Principles of Project Delivery

Project delivery is guided by the following principles:
- Alignment with organisational objectives and IMS requirements
- Clear definition of scope, deliverables, and success criteria
- Proactive risk management and mitigation
- Integration of quality, environmental, information security, and safety controls
- Structured monitoring, reporting, and governance
- Continual improvement and lessons learned

⸻

## 4. Project Lifecycle Phases

The organisation adopts a PMP-aligned lifecycle consisting of five key phases:

### 4.1 Initiation
- Define project objectives, scope, and feasibility
- Identify stakeholders, roles, and responsibilities
- Conduct preliminary risk assessment
- Obtain formal approval to proceed

### 4.2 Planning
- Develop a detailed project plan including schedule, budget, resources, and milestones
- Identify and assess project risks and mitigation strategies
- Define quality, environmental, information security, and H&S requirements
- Establish communication and reporting plans
- Review and approve project plans before execution

### 4.3 Execution
- Allocate resources and implement planned activities
- Manage project scope, schedule, and budget
- Communicate with stakeholders as per the communication plan
- Apply IMS controls consistently across project activities
- Monitor and control risks, issues, and changes

### 4.4 Monitoring and Controlling
- Track progress against project plan, KPIs, and IMS requirements
- Identify and resolve variances or deviations
- Apply corrective actions where needed
- Manage change requests through formal processes
- Maintain documentation and records for audit and review

### 4.5 Closing
- Verify completion of deliverables and project objectives
- Obtain formal client or stakeholder acceptance
- Conduct post-project review and capture lessons learned
- Archive project records in accordance with IMS document control procedures
- Release resources and formally close the project

⸻

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities
- Project Sponsor / Senior Management: Provides strategic direction, approval, and oversight
- Project Manager: Responsible for planning, execution, monitoring, and closure
- Project Team: Executes tasks, reports progress, and adheres to IMS requirements
- IMS Lead: Provides guidance on integration of IMS standards into project delivery
- Stakeholders: Participate in project reviews and approvals as appropriate

⸻

## 6. Risk and Quality Management
- Project risks are identified, assessed, and monitored throughout the lifecycle
- Quality assurance measures are embedded into deliverables and processes
- Environmental, information security, and health & safety risks are managed in line with IMS controls
- Corrective actions are implemented and reviewed for effectiveness

⸻

## 7. Communication and Reporting
- Regular status updates are provided to stakeholders
- Issues, changes, and risks are escalated according to agreed thresholds
- Project documentation is maintained in controlled systems
- Lessons learned are communicated and integrated into future projects

⸻

## 8. Documentation and Records

Project records include:
- Project charter, plans, and schedules
- Risk assessments and mitigation logs
- Meeting minutes, progress reports, and change requests
- Quality, H&S, environmental, and security checklists
- Lessons learned and closure reports

All records are maintained under IMS document and records control procedures.

⸻

## 9. Review and Continual Improvement
- Project performance is reviewed during and after delivery
- Lessons learned are incorporated into future planning and IMS updates
- Trends, recurring issues, and improvement opportunities are reported to senior management
