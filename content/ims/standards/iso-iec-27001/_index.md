# IT Security Management System (ISMS)

## Introduction

An Information Security Management System (ISMS), aligned with ISO/IEC 27001, provides a structured framework to manage sensitive information, ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability. It helps organisations:

- Secure information in all forms.
- Improve resilience to cyber threats.
- Adapt to evolving risks.
- Reduce costs from information security breaches.

## 1. Scope

This ISMS applies to all Green Orbit Digital operations, including:

- Digital infrastructure and internal systems.
- Partner ecosystems and vendor interactions.
- Client-facing services and communications.

It establishes systematic processes to manage information securely and responsibly.

## 2. Normative References

- **ISO/IEC 27001** – Information Security Management System requirements.  
- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework** – Structured risk assessment and control guidelines.  
- **GDPR & UK Data Protection Act** – Legal compliance for personal data.  
- **ITIL Service Management Best Practices** – Ensures secure and efficient IT operations.  
- **Cyber Essentials, PCI-DSS, ISO/IEC 27001 controls** – Industry best practices.  

Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action, contract termination, or legal consequences.

## 3. Terms and Definitions

- **Confidentiality:** Ensuring information is accessible only to authorised individuals.  
- **Integrity:** Maintaining accuracy and reliability of data.  
- **Availability:** Ensuring information is accessible when required.  
- **Users:** Employees, contractors, suppliers, clients, or partners accessing systems.  
- **Systems:** IT equipment, software, and connected communication tools.  
- **Data:** Any electronic information.  
- **Data Store:** Devices or cloud storage for data.  
- **Password:** Sequence used for authentication.

## 4. Context of the Organisation

### 4.1 Internal and External Context

**Internal:**  
- Agile, remote-first structure with cloud-based tools.  
- Mission: Integrate sustainability and technology into digital strategies.  
- Infrastructure secured via cloud-native solutions with redundancy.  
- Values: Transparency, ethical conduct, and sustainable innovation.

**External:**  
- Sectors: Digital sustainability, marketing, space innovation.  
- Regulatory: GDPR, UK DPA, Computer Misuse Act, AI and digital compliance.  
- Cyber threats: Phishing, social engineering, SaaS vulnerabilities.

### 4.2 Interested Parties

- **Clients & partners** – Confidentiality, transparency, security assurances.  
- **Regulators & auditors** – Compliance evidence.  
- **Employees & contractors** – Clear roles and responsibilities.  
- **Cloud providers & vendors** – Secure and compliant services.  
- **Industry bodies** – Alignment with standards (ISO, Clean Creatives, Ecologi).  

Requirements are addressed via policies, risk assessments, and ISMS controls.

### 4.3 ISMS Scope

Applies to all business areas including:

- Client-facing and internal services.  
- Data storage, processing, and transmission.  
- Collaboration tools and analytics systems.  
- Third-party interactions.  

Scope is reviewed annually.

### 4.4 ISMS Overview

- Documented risk assessment and treatment methodology.  
- Integrated policies, defined roles, and responsibilities.  
- Continuous monitoring, audits, and feedback loops.  
- Risk identification, assessment, evaluation, and treatment (mitigate, transfer, accept, avoid).

## 5. Leadership

### 5.1 Leadership & Commitment

Top management ensures:

- Alignment of ISMS with strategic goals.  
- Integration of ISMS into business processes.  
- Allocation of resources and personnel for ISMS operations.  
- Communication of security importance.  
- Monitoring and continual improvement of ISMS.

### 5.2 Information Security Policy

Policy commitments include:

- Alignment with purpose, strategy, and legal obligations.  
- Measurable objectives and continual improvement.  
- Communication and availability to interested parties.

**Supporting Policies:**

1. Access Control Policy  
2. Asset Management Policy  
3. Data Classification and Handling Policy  
4. Cryptographic Policy  
5. Remote Work & BYOD Policy  
6. Acceptable Use Policy  
7. Supplier Security Policy  
8. Incident Management Policy  
9. Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery Policy  
10. Legal, Regulatory & Compliance Policy  

Policies are reviewed annually and stored centrally.

### 5.3 Organisational Roles

Defined roles include:

- Director, Information Security Officer (ISO), Data Protection Officer (DPO), and department heads.  
- Responsibilities, authorities, and accountability documented in the ISMS Responsibility Matrix.

## 6. Planning

### 6.1 Actions to Address Risks & Opportunities

- Identify risks and opportunities based on context and interested parties.  
- Integrate actions into ISMS processes and evaluate effectiveness.  

**Risk Assessment Process:**

- Identify assets, threats, vulnerabilities.  
- Assess likelihood and impact.  
- Assign ownership and prioritise risks.  

**Risk Treatment:**  
- Mitigation, transfer, acceptance, or avoidance.  
- Controls aligned with ISO/IEC 27001 Annex A.  
- Statement of Applicability (SoA) maintained.

### 6.2 Information Security Objectives

- Objectives aligned with policy, SMART, and legally compliant.  
- Responsibilities, resources, timelines, and KPIs defined.  
- Monitored via dashboards and internal reporting.

### 6.3 Planning of Changes

- Planned changes assessed for security impact.  
- Documented, communicated, and monitored.  
- Maintains ISMS integrity.

## 7. Support

### 7.1 Resources

- Personnel, budget, technical infrastructure, and cloud platforms.  

### 7.2 Competence

- Staff trained and assessed.  
- Role-based specialized training.  
- Competence records maintained.

### 7.3 Awareness

- Organisation-wide understanding of responsibilities and policies.  
- Simulated exercises, communications, and knowledge base support.

### 7.4 Communication

- Defined internal and external channels.  
- Regular updates and tested incident communication.

### 7.5 Documented Information

- Maintained in Notion and Proton Drive.  
- Version-controlled, reviewed, and protected.  
- Includes required ISO 27001 documentation, procedures, and optional supporting documents.

## 8. Operation

### 8.1 Operational Planning & Control

- Criteria established for all processes impacting security.  
- Controls implemented (technical, physical, procedural).  
- Documented evidence maintained.  
- Managed change control for planned/unplanned changes.  
- Third-party services controlled via due diligence, DPAs, and SLAs.

### 8.2 Information Security Risk Assessment

- Conducted annually and upon significant changes.  
- Documented in the Risk Register.

### 8.3 Risk Treatment

- Controls implemented per SoA.  
- Responsibilities, timelines, and resources assigned.  
- Outcomes logged and reviewed.  
- Alignment with sustainability KPIs.

## 9. Performance Evaluation

### 9.1 Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis & Evaluation

- Track incident response, policy compliance, and control effectiveness.  
- Automated tools, manual checks, dashboards, and reporting.  
- Documented in Notion.

### 9.2 Internal Audit

- Annual or as-needed audits.  
- Independent review of ISMS conformity.  
- Audit programme defines scope, methods, responsibilities, and planning.  
- Findings logged, corrective actions tracked.

### 9.3 Management Review

- At planned intervals (at least annually).  
- Inputs: previous actions, context changes, stakeholder feedback, audit outcomes, risk status.  
- Outputs: decisions on improvements, policy updates, objectives, resources, emerging risks.

## 10. Improvement

### 10.1 Continual Improvement

- Reviews KPIs, risk assessments, audits, incidents.  
- Integrates feedback from stakeholders.  
- Adapts to emerging risks, technology, and legal requirements.  
- Documented and tracked in Notion.

### 10.2 Nonconformity & Corrective Action

- Identification, evaluation, corrective action, validation, and closure.  
- Lessons learned incorporated into audits, training, and policies.  
- Third-party controls reviewed and enforced.
